Welcome to my website!! Very glad that you are here.

I am an aspiring material scientist presently pursuing my MSc and eagerly looking forward to start my PhD studies soon. Having embarked on this exciting journey back in 2016 as a bachelor student in India, materials have since tickled my curiosity with their fascinating, mysterious and yet useful properties.

Hence, through my research I want to contribute towards a better fundamental understanding of materials, how they work and thus help in solving some of the pressing problems that mankind faces today. Currently, I am pursuing MSc from RWTH Aachen university where I work with both computational as well as experimental techniques. I am expecting to complete my masters soon to start on a journey towards obtaining a PhD.

I have always enjoyed mathematics and physics since my high school days. As an material science student I especially find the atomic and sub-atomic scale very interesting. Solid-state physics, physical metallurgy, plasticity of materials, nano-characterization techniques and atomistic simulations are some of my favourite areas in the field of material science.

Currently, I am working with molecular dynamics and monte-carlo simulations to investigate segregation behaviour of grain boundaries in different alloys. I also work on low temperature thermo-electric materials to demystify the role of GBs in improving the figure of merit in these materials.

Please feel free to contact me if you are interested to know more about the exciting work that I do!
